2Matthias Oschinski

3Beth Noveck

Founder, InnovateUS · Former U.S. Deputy CTO
Intro line

Beth is the Founder of InnovateUS and former United States Deputy Chief Technology Officer in the Obama administration. Her organization trains public-sector employees across the country in AI and digital skills, free of charge.

Her lens

National policy. Cross-state benchmarks. Public-sector workforce capability. Give her room without letting her absorb the panel.

Question 1

Opener Round-Robin 9 min

Order: Chelsea → Matthias → Beth · 3 min each

In one specific story or example: what is something happening right now in DC, on the ground, that tells you we are either ahead of or behind where we need to be on AI and workforce?

Why this works: forces specificity, blocks the stump speech, surfaces real DC signal for the AI Task Force in the room.

Questions 2 to 4

One Per Panelist 9 min

To Chelsea
DC Public Library is one of the few institutions in the District that touches every Ward. What are you seeing at the branch level that policymakers in this room are probably missing about how residents actually engage with AI?
To Matthias
You study which skills are gaining and losing value. If a DC resident walked into a library tomorrow and asked "what should I learn to be employable in three years," what is the honest answer, and what is the answer we are afraid to give?
To Beth
InnovateUS trains public-sector workers across multiple states. What is one thing other governments are doing that DC should steal, and one thing DC is doing that you would recommend to others?
Question 5

Closing Round-Robin 3 min

1 min each · Suggested order: Beth → Matthias → Chelsea

If everyone in this room could do one thing differently on Monday morning, what would you ask them to do?

Action-oriented. Gives the AI Advisory Group and Task Force a tangible takeaway. Ending on Chelsea anchors the panel in community.
